Sewing

Sewing has been a passion of mine since I was little. When I was young, I often hand-sewed items, even if they weren’t the best quality, since I didn’t have access to a sewing machine and didn’t know how to use one. One Christmas, I was excited to see a product called “Sew Cool” that claimed to sew without thread. I was thrilled and didn’t bother reading the instructions, thinking I could figure it out like the commercials showed. Unfortunately, I broke it almost immediately, and I was devastated. I remember bawling over it because I was so excited to finally sew. Not long after that incident, my mom saved up and bought me a real sewing machine for my birthday in January. I thought it was the coolest gift ever! My aunt promised to help me learn how to use it, but life got busy, and it sat unused for years.

Then came COVID-19. During the lockdown, my mom had the idea to make masks, and I was finally old enough to look up instructions to use the sewing machine myself. To my surprise, it was fairly simple! That experience reignited my passion for sewing and gave me the confidence to tackle new projects. Since then, I’ve completed a few fun sewing projects.

One of my favorite projects was upcycling a pair of pants that were too small. I cut open the sides and added fabric to create stylish bellbottoms. I had so much fun with that project that I did the same with another pair of pants! It felt great to give them a new life and make something that fit me better. I’ve also kept up my hand stitching. Last year, around Halloween, I made a vampire bat and a little ghost for my boyfriend. These small projects are a great way to express my creativity. Sewing has become an important hobby for me, and I look forward to creating more projects in the future. Each piece I make reminds me of how far I’ve come from those early days of hand sewing and the joy that comes from making something with my own hands.
