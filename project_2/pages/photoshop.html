My Photoshop Journey

My journey into digital editing started back in high school. During my junior year, I took an online class over Zoom that introduced me to a program called Photopea. Since we were learning from home during COVID-19, Photopea worked as a stand-in for Photoshop, which the school couldn’t provide. I found myself really enjoying digital editing and learning all the different tools. The gingerbread house showcased on the left is one project I completed in that class.
In my senior year, I took a photography class and used Photopea again. This time, I made bigger projects, diving deeper into digital image editing. After graduation, I put digital art on hold for a while, but I came back to it when I joined a play at McHenry County College. I wanted to make some mock ads for my social media, so I opened up Photopea again—and I was hooked! I kept building my skills and experimenting with different styles and effects.
Around that time, our director, Jay Geller, noticed my work and started asking me to create “Meet The Cast” posts for the MCC BlackBox social media pages. Working on these projects inspired me to pursue digital art more seriously and consider a career in graphic design. Now, I use Photoshop for my college classes and love working on creative projects whenever I can. This journey has been a lot of fun, and I’m excited to keep learning and improving my Photoshop skills!
