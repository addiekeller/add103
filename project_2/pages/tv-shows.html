My Top TV Shows

The Owl House - Disney

One of my favorite shows is The Owl House, a Disney Channel cartoon that came out a few years ago. This story follows a human named Luz who stumbles into the Demon Realm and discovers a world full of magic and mystery. Luz, although human, starts learning witchcraft and forms strong friendships with unique characters like her mentor Eda the Owl Lady and King, a tiny but mighty creature. As the series unfolds, Luz becomes a hero, standing up to protect the Boiling Isles from dark forces. I watched The Owl House on my own first and loved it so much that I watched it again with my boyfriend, who enjoyed it just as much.

Star vs. The Forces of Evil - Disney

Another show I love is Star vs. The Forces of Evil, an older Disney Channel show. This story centers around Star Butterfly, a princess from another dimension who lives on Earth as an exchange student. Star is both powerful and funny, but as the series goes on, she realizes that being a princess is a huge responsibility. She has to confront issues from her kingdom’s past and even deals with difficult topics like inequality and prejudice. Star’s friendship with Marco, her Earth friend, gets complicated too, with some ups and downs in her relationships with him and her ex-boyfriend, Tom. Star vs. The Forces of Evil mixes fantasy, humor, and real-life challenges in a way that's exciting to watch.

Adventure Time - Cartoon Network

Adventure Time is another favorite, though it took me a while to get hooked. It starts off with silly, stand-alone episodes, but over time, it reveals a deeper, complex story. The show follows Finn the Human and Jake the Dog as they explore the Land of Ooo, which is full of bizarre creatures and kingdoms. Eventually, Adventure Time introduces themes like loss, friendship, and even includes LGBTQ+ representation, which was rare for its time. Even after it ended, they created extra episodes and a spin-off called Fionna and Cake, which adds new layers to the original world and characters.
