Instruments I Can Actually Play

The two instruments I still play today are the ukulele and the guitar. My journey with instruments began in middle school when I discovered the ukulele. I was instantly hooked! I begged my mom to buy me one, and once I got it, I couldn’t stop playing. During the COVID-19 lockdown in 2020, I picked up the guitar, and since then, I’ve been writing songs for both instruments. Though I started with the ukulele, the guitar has become my main focus now, and it’s an instrument I’m really passionate about.

Instruments That Came up in School, but Never Really Stuck

Aside from the ukulele and guitar, I’ve played a few other instruments, though I didn’t stick with them for very long. In 5th grade music class, we were required to buy harmonicas and recorders to use in class, and while it was fun, I never really pursued them beyond that year. I still have those instruments, but they’re more of a memory than anything I play regularly. During that same year, I joined the 5th grade band and chose to play the flute. They told us it was one of the harder instruments, but I managed to make the right sound on my first try, so I felt like it was meant to be! I enjoyed playing the flute that year, but I didn’t continue with it in middle or high school.
When I got to high school, I decided to try piano and took a class for it. I was doing pretty well at first, but unfortunately, COVID-19 interrupted the semester, so I never continued. I know how to read sheet music, so I could theoretically pick it up again if I wanted to, but I haven’t had the motivation to go back to it.
